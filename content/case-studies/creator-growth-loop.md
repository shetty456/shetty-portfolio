# Designing Yelow's Creator Growth Loop

## Context
Yelow was scaling its creator base but acquisition was expensive ($180 CAC for a creator) and largely paid. We needed a viral mechanism to drive organic growth.

## Problem Definition
Creators trust other creators. The best advocate for Yelow is a creator who's already making money on the platform. But we had no systematic way to harness this.

## The Insight
During user interviews, I kept hearing: "I heard about Yelow from [friend/peer creator]." Referrals were already happening organically — we just weren't incentivizing or measuring them.

## Designing the Loop

**Trigger**: Creator reaches their first $100 revenue milestone
**Action**: In-app prompt to "Invite a fellow creator"
**Reward**: Both parties unlock 2 months of premium features (normally $29/month)
**Viral Coefficient**: Each creator who receives an invite has 40% conversion to activated creator

The key design decision: timing the trigger at the $100 milestone (not immediately) because:
- Creator has validated the platform value
- They're in a positive emotional state
- They have credibility to share with peers

## Measurement

Metrics tracked:
- K-factor (viral coefficient)
- Invite send rate at trigger point
- Invite conversion rate
- LTV of referred creators vs paid-acquired creators

## Results
- **K-factor: 0.4** (40% viral growth multiplier)
- **500+ creator acquisitions per month** within 60 days of launch
- Referred creators had **35% higher 6-month retention** than paid-acquired
- Referred creators reached $100 MRR milestone 40% faster (peer guidance effect)
- Acquisition cost via referral: **$12 vs $180** (paid)

## Learnings
1. Timing matters more than incentive size — the $100 milestone was crucial
2. Peer credibility > platform credibility for creator acquisition
3. Mutual benefit (both parties win) drives higher share rates than one-sided rewards
