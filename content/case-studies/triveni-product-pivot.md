# From B2C to B2B: How Data Led Triveni's Product Pivot

## The Problem
Triveni launched as a B2C platform targeting individual users. Growth was decent — 100k users in 12 months — but unit economics were concerning. CAC was high, LTV was low, and churn was significant.

## Discovery Phase
Through cohort analysis, I noticed a pattern: a small segment of users (about 8%) were using the platform in groups — sharing accounts, collaborating on features designed for individuals.

This wasn't intended behavior. But these "power users" had:
- 3x higher retention (D90 retention: 68% vs 22% for individual users)
- 5x higher average revenue per account
- Near-zero churn after Month 3

## The Hypothesis
These were likely small businesses using our tool. If we built explicitly for them, we could 10x our LTV while cutting CAC (B2B sales is more efficient at scale).

## Building the Case
Over 3 months, I:
- Interviewed 25 of these "power users" — confirmed 80% were small businesses
- Modeled LTV scenarios for B2B vs B2C
- Ran a scrappy "B2B beta" with 10 companies using our existing product + white-glove support
- B2B beta NPS was 71 vs 34 for B2C

## The Pivot
Presented findings to CEO and board. Key artifacts:
- Cohort analysis showing LTV differential
- B2B beta results
- 12-month revenue model

Board approved the pivot. Execution involved:
- New pricing tiers (team seats model)
- Feature reprioritization (collaboration, admin controls, reporting)
- Sales motion from PLG to sales-assisted for enterprise tier

## Result
- ARR grew **180%** in 12 months post-pivot
- Net Revenue Retention: 118% (expansion > churn)
- Team size grew from 12 to 28 to support growth
